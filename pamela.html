Pamela Zamora is a doctoral candidate in Hispanic Studies at the University of British Columbia. Her dissertation revolves around how 20th and 21st Central American and Mexican fiction contests humanitarian crises such feminicido, indigenous genocide and forced migration in the neoliberal epoch. At the intersection of sound studies, feminist theory and migration studies, her work amines to trace the literary representation of those crises. Pamela collected primary and critical sources to develop an exhaustive bibliography database on female writers from the Golden Age. Profile: https://fhis.ubc.ca/profile/pamela-zamora-quesada/
